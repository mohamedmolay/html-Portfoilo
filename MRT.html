Introduction to MTR

Mauritania is a country located in Northwest Africa, bordered by the Atlantic Ocean to the west, Western Sahara to the north, Algeria to the northeast, Mali to the east and southeast, and Senegal to the southwest.
It serves as a cultural and geographical bridge between North Africa and Sub-Saharan Africa.
The capital city, Nouakchott, lies on the Atlantic coast and is the political and economic center of the country. Mauritania covers a vast area of mostly desert land, with the Sahara occupying much of its territory, giving it a hot and dry climate.

The population of Mauritania is diverse, made up of Arab-Berber and Sub-Saharan African groups, which together form a unique blend of cultures, languages, and traditions.
Arabic is the official language, and Islam is the state religion, shaping much of the country's social and cultural life.
Mauritania’s economy is based largely on mining, fishing, and agriculture, with iron ore being one of its main exports. Despite economic challenges, Mauritania is known for its rich cultural heritage, hospitality, and traditional way of life, which continues to reflect the country’s deep historical roots.
